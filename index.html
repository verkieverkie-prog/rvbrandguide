You have done the research. It made things worse.

You have watched the videos, read the forums, and asked the owners groups. One reviewer swears by a brand. The next one calls the same brand junk. Both sound sure of themselves. That is not because you are researching wrong. It is because most reviews rate a badge, and a badge is not a fixed thing.

An RV brand can change owners twice, change factories once, and cut the wall material mid-year, all without changing the name on the side. The 2018 unit earned its reputation. The 2021 unit with the same name is a different machine from a different assembly line. Flat answers about brands fail because the truth splits by model year.

The record that does not have an opinion

There is one source that does not care about sponsorships, dealer margins, or brand loyalty: the federal record. Recalls, investigations, class actions, and warranty documents are all public, all dated, and all traceable to specific model years. Almost nobody reads them, because they are scattered across databases and written in regulatory language.

That is the entire method of this guide. We pulled the record for every brand we cover, matched it to model years, and turned it into three plain calls: buy, caution, or avoid. Every call shows the reason and the source, so you can check our work.
